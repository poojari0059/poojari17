                                                                    <b><h1><center>CRUNCHBOX</center></h1></b>
   <b><h1>CUT IT</h1></b>
<font face="arial">
<font size="4">
<p>
   
</p>
You have been given a red paper of size (<b>M</b>*<b>N</b>) which is a combination of square papers as shown in the figure below. You have to cut the paper to get each square of size (<b>1</b>*<b>1</b>) separately with minimum cost. The rules for cutting the red paper are as follows :
<lo>
<li> You can't fold the paper and at a time only one layer of paper can be cut.</li>
<li> Paper can be cut along horizontal or vertical direction only as indicated by the black line. </li>
<li>Cost of  vertical cuts are <b> x<sub>1</sub>, x<sub>2</sub>,......., x<sub>M-1.</sub></b></li>
<li>Cost of horizontal cuts are <b> y<sub>1</sub>, y<sub>2</sub>, ......., y<sub>N-1.</sub></b></li>
<li>Cost of cutting the whole paper into squares of size(<b>1</b>*<b>1</b>) is the sum of the cost of successive cuts. </li>
</ol>  
<h4>Red Paper</h4>
<img src="ques/cho.png" alt="Mountain View" style="width:304px;height:228px;"><br><br>
<b>Input Format</b><br>
The first line of input contains a single integer <b>T</b>, denoting the number of test cases. <br>
Each test case contains <b>3</b> lines describing a test case.<br>
1.<b>M</b> and <b>N</b> are the horizontal and vertical dimensions respectively of the paper.<br>
2.<b>M</b> space separated integer denoting the cost of vertical cuts.<br>
3.<b>N</b> space separated integer denoting the cost of  horizontal cuts.<br><br>
<b>Constraints</b>
<ul>
<li>1<=<b>T</b><=10<sup>2</sup></li>
<li>2<=<b>M,N</b><=10<sup>2</sup></li>
<li>0<<b>x<sub>i</sub>,y<sub>i</sub></b><=10<sup>2</sup></li>
</ul>
<b>Output Format</b><br>
For each test case, print minimum cost to cut the paper to get each square separately in a new line.<br><br>
<b>Sample Input</b> <br>
1<br>
4 3<br>
1 4 3<br>
2 5<br><br>

<b>Sample output</b><br>
28<br><br>
